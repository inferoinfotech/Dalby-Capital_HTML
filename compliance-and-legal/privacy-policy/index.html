Protecting Your Personal
Information Matters to Us

We’re committed to handling your data with care, transparency and in full compliance with Australian privacy laws.

Introduction

Your Privacy, Our Priority

At Dalby Capital, we understand the importance of safeguarding your personal information. Whether you're engaging with us online, over the phone or in person, we want you to feel confident that your data is being collected, used and stored responsibly.

This Privacy Policy outlines how we manage your personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). It applies to all individuals who interact with our services—including clients, website visitors, prospective clients and other stakeholders.

By continuing to engage with Dalby Capital, you consent to the practices described in this policy.

What Personal Information We Collect

The Types of Data We May Hold About You

We collect a range of personal and sensitive information to provide tailored financial services and meet our legal obligations. This may include:

  • Full name, date of birth and contact details
  • Residential and postal address
  • Identification documents (e.g., driver's licence, passport)
  • Tax file number (TFN) and financial details
  • Employment, income and investment history
  • Information about your risk profile, goals and financial objectives
  • Records of your communications and interactions with us

We only collect what’s necessary to deliver our services effectively and legally.

How We Collect Your Information

Direct, Secure and With Your Consent

We typically collect your personal information directly from you when you:

  • Fill out forms or applications
  • Use our client portal
  • Meet with an adviser or contact us
  • Subscribe to our services or newsletters
  • Interact with us via phone, email or online channels

In some cases, we may also receive information from third parties such as your accountant, legal representative, financial institutions, or public databases—always with your knowledge or consent where required.

Why We Collect and Use Your Information

To Deliver Services and Meet Regulatory Standards

We use your personal information to:

  • Establish your identity and verify your account
  • Provide tailored financial advice and portfolio management
  • Conduct anti-money laundering (AML) and know-your-customer (KYC) checks
  • Manage your investments and transactions
  • Communicate with you about your account, reports or market updates
  • Meet legal and compliance obligations

Your information also helps us improve our services, prevent fraud, and personalise your experience with us.

Disclosure of Personal Information

We Share Responsibly and Only When Necessary

We may disclose your personal information to:

  • Third-party service providers who assist in delivering our services (e.g., IT support, platform providers, auditors)
  • Government agencies, regulators or law enforcement (e.g., ASIC, AUSTRAC, ATO) when required by law
  • Professional advisers acting on your behalf (e.g., accountants, lawyers)
  • Our related entities or business partners, where permitted and relevant

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties. Every party we share information with is bound by confidentiality and privacy obligations.

How We Store and Protect Your Data

Secure Systems, Safeguards and Monitoring

We take extensive steps to ensure your personal information is protected from unauthorised access, loss, misuse or alteration. These include:

  • Secure encryption and firewalls across all digital platforms
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for client portal access
  • Role-based access controls and staff training
  • Physical safeguards in offices and data storage centres
  • Regular audits and reviews of our data protection measures

When personal information is no longer needed, we securely destroy or de-identify it in accordance with Australian laws.

Overseas Disclosure of Information

When Data Leaves Australia

In some cases, your information may be disclosed or stored overseas—for example, where we use secure international cloud-based systems or engage global service providers.

These disclosures are made only where:

  • It is necessary for service delivery
  • Data protection standards are deemed equivalent or satisfactory
  • Contracts are in place to ensure compliance with Australian privacy standards

We carefully assess all overseas data arrangements to protect your privacy.

Accessing and Updating Your Information

You Have the Right to Know and Correct

You can request access to the personal information we hold about you at any time. You also have the right to request corrections if any details are inaccurate, incomplete or outdated.

To make a request, simply contact our Privacy Officer using the details at the bottom of this page. We’ll respond within a reasonable timeframe and explain any decisions or limitations, as required by law.

Cookies, Tracking and Online Data

How We Use Web Technologies

When you visit our website or client portal, we may use cookies and other tracking technologies to improve your online experience. These help us:

  • Understand how you use the site
  • Remember your preferences
  • Provide secure and consistent access
  • Tailor content and offers to your interests

You can manage your cookie settings in your browser or refer to our separate [Cookie Policy] for more detail.

aking a Privacy Complaint

We Take Your Concerns Seriously

If you believe your privacy has been compromised or mishandled, we encourage you to contact us directly. Our team is committed to addressing your concerns promptly and transparently.
